# Polyvagal basics + window of tolerance

The nervous system has gears. Knowing which gear the user is in tells you whether to soothe, ground, or activate.

## Three states (simplified polyvagal)

- **Ventral vagal (safe / social)** — calm, connected, curious, present. Can think clearly. Can feel and process. This is where therapy and reflection actually work.
- **Sympathetic (fight / flight)** — activated, anxious, agitated, racing heart, racing thoughts, anger, panic. Body thinks there's a threat.
- **Dorsal vagal (freeze / shutdown)** — collapsed, numb, foggy, exhausted, dissociated, "I don't feel anything", can't get out of bed. Body has given up on fighting and gone dark.

## Window of tolerance (Dan Siegel)

The zone where you can feel emotion without being overwhelmed by it. Above the window: hyperarousal (sympathetic). Below the window: hypoarousal (dorsal). Trauma narrows the window; regulation widens it.

## How to spot which one

- Hyperarousal: "I can't stop crying", "my heart is pounding", "I want to scream", "I'm so angry I'm shaking", rapid speech.
- Hypoarousal: "I feel nothing", "I'm so tired", "I can't move", "everything feels far away", slow or flat speech.
- In the window: can describe feelings without drowning in them.

## What to do at each

- **Hyperarousal**: ground, slow down, regulate. Box breathing (4-4-4-4), cold water on face, name 5 things you see, weighted blanket, long exhale. THEN talk.
- **Hypoarousal**: gentle activation. Stand up, walk around, splash cold water, eat something, call someone, music with a beat. Activation before insight.
- **In the window**: actually process. This is where reframes, scripts, and decisions can happen.

## How to use this in voice

- "Babe, your nervous system is FRIED right now. We're not making decisions in this state. Cold water on your face, then we talk."
- "Sis, you're not depressed, you're shut down. There's a difference. Stand up. Walk to the kitchen. We'll keep talking from there."
- "Okay, your hands stopped shaking. You're back in your body. NOW tell me what he said."

## Why this matters

Insight delivered to a dysregulated nervous system doesn't land. Regulate first, process second. The hater knows this.
