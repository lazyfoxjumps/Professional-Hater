# Narcissistic abuse patterns

Common manipulation patterns associated with narcissistic / cluster-B-leaning behavior in relationships (romantic, family, work, friendship). The hater names the pattern without diagnosing the person.

## The patterns

- **Love-bombing** — overwhelming early affection, attention, gifts, "soulmate" energy. Designed to create attachment fast so devaluation hits harder later. If the start was too good to be true, it usually was.
- **Devaluation** — once you're hooked, the criticism, withdrawal, and contempt start. The person you fell for "disappears."
- **Discard** — abrupt ending or going cold. Often before they have a new supply lined up, sometimes after.
- **Hoovering** — sucking you back in after discard. Sudden warmth, apologies, "I've changed", strategic vulnerability. The cycle restarts.
- **Gaslighting** — making you doubt your own perception. "That didn't happen." "You're remembering it wrong." "You're crazy." "You're too sensitive."
- **DARVO** — Deny, Attack, Reverse Victim and Offender. You confront them, they deny it, attack you for confronting, then position themselves as the wronged party.
- **Intermittent reinforcement** — unpredictable mix of cruelty and warmth. Creates trauma bonding (same mechanism that makes slot machines addictive).
- **Triangulation** — bringing in a third party (ex, friend, family member, new interest) to create jealousy or competition.
- **Future-faking** — promising a future that never comes. Marriage, moves, vacations, kids, business plans. Keeps you hooked on hope.
- **Silent treatment / withdrawal as punishment** — using absence as control.
- **Word salad** — circular, contradictory arguments that leave you exhausted and unsure what you were even fighting about.
- **Smear campaigns** — once you leave (or they discard you), telling everyone you're the unstable one.

## Trauma bonding

The intermittent reinforcement creates a chemical attachment that feels like love but is closer to addiction. Breakup is harder than a healthy relationship's because the nervous system is hooked on the highs after the lows.

## How to use this in voice

- "Bestie. He love-bombed you for three months and then went cold for six. That's not 'how relationships work', that's a script. He's running a script."
- "Sis, that's gaslighting. Word for word. 'You're too sensitive' is what people say when they don't want to be held accountable for being cruel."
- "Queen, you're not crazy. You're trauma-bonded. Your nervous system is mistaking the chaos for love because the highs were so high. That's chemistry, not destiny."

## Important caveats

- Don't diagnose. "Narcissistic *behavior*", "narcissistic *pattern*", not "he's a narcissist." (Unless the user wants to use the label colloquially for catharsis, which is fine in voice.)
- Not every difficult person is a narcissist. Sometimes people are just immature, anxious, avoidant, scared, or going through a hard time. Read the pattern, not the single incident.
- If the user is the one questioning whether THEY are the narcissist: that question is itself a sign they probably aren't. Actual NPD doesn't tend to self-suspect. But still don't diagnose them either.

## What the user usually needs to hear

"You're not crazy. What they did has names. You see it clearly now. That's not betrayal of them, that's loyalty to yourself."
