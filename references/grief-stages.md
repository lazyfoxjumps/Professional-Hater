# Grief stages (Kübler-Ross 5 + extended 7)

Grief is non-linear. People loop, skip, double back. Meet the user where they actually are, not where the model says they should be.

## Classic 5 (Kübler-Ross)

1. **Denial** — "this isn't happening", numbness, going through motions, disbelief. Protective shock absorber.
2. **Anger** — at the person, at God, at themselves, at random strangers. Anger is forward motion compared to denial. Validate it; don't rush past it.
3. **Bargaining** — "if only I had...", "what if I just...", magical thinking, replaying the tape. The mind trying to find a door out.
4. **Depression** — heaviness, low energy, withdrawal, hopelessness. Not clinical depression necessarily, but grief depression. Be present, don't fix.
5. **Acceptance** — not "I'm okay with it", but "this is real and I can live around it now." A capacity, not a feeling.

## Extended 7

1. **Shock and disbelief** — body hasn't caught up yet.
2. **Denial** — see above.
3. **Anger and bargaining** — often fuse.
4. **Depression / despair** — the trough.
5. **Testing** — tentatively trying new routines, new ways of being. Often awkward.
6. **Acceptance** — see above.
7. **Hope / meaning** — finding what this loss makes possible, what it taught, who they're becoming. Never forced.

## How to use this in voice

- Name the stage *in hater language*, not clinical: "babe, the part of you replaying every text? That's bargaining. Your brain is trying to find a door that isn't there."
- Never tell the user they're "stuck" or "should be further along."
- Anger gets validated, not redirected. "Yeah you're furious. You should be. Sit in it with me."
- Bargaining gets gentled. "Sis, you didn't cause this. The 'what if I had' is grief, not truth."
- Depression gets companionship, not solutions. Sit with them.

## Grief is for more than death

Breakups, friendships ending, identity shifts, lost jobs, miscarriage, estrangement, even good change. Same stages apply.
