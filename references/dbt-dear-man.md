# DBT DEAR MAN

Marsha Linehan's script for assertive communication. Useful when the user is about to have a hard conversation, set a boundary verbally, or confront someone and wants a structure that holds up under pressure.

## DEAR (what to say)

- **D**escribe — the situation, in facts. "When you canceled on me three times in a row..."
- **E**xpress — your feelings, using I-statements. "I felt unimportant and dismissed."
- **A**ssert — what you want or need, directly. "I need you to commit when you make plans, or tell me upfront you can't."
- **R**einforce — why it's worth it for them too. "When you do that, I trust you more and I'm easier to make plans with."

## MAN (how to say it)

- **M**indful — stay on point. Don't get derailed into old fights or side issues. Broken-record the main ask if needed.
- **A**ppear confident — eye contact, steady voice, no hedging language ("um, maybe, sort of, I guess").
- **N**egotiate — be willing to flex on the how, not the what. The core need stays. The execution can move.

## When to use it

- Hard conversations the user is dreading
- Confronting someone who tends to derail
- Saying no to something they usually say yes to
- Asking for something they usually avoid asking for

## When NOT to use it

- With actively abusive people. DEAR MAN assumes the other person is capable of basic respect. If they're not, the user needs to leave the conversation, not negotiate inside it.
- In acute emotional dysregulation. Regulate first, script second.
- For boundaries that don't require the other person's input (those are just statements about what YOU do).

## How to use this in voice

The hater walks the user through the script.

- "Okay bestie, let's write this out together. What's the FACT he keeps doing? Just the fact. No 'always' no 'never'."
- "Now: how did it make you feel? One feeling word. Not 'fine'. Not 'whatever'. Real one."
- "What do you want? Specifically. Not 'be better.' Like, what action."
- "And what's in it for him to actually do this? Because we want this to land, not just feel good to say."
- "Read it back to me. Out loud. We're going to rehearse this so you don't fold the second he looks at you."

## Sample DEAR MAN, in hater style

User wants to confront a friend who keeps making plans then bailing.

"Babe, try this:
'When you bail on plans last minute, three times in a row, [Describe]
I feel like I'm not actually a priority to you, even though I keep showing up. [Express]
I need you to either commit when we make plans, or be upfront that you can't, instead of canceling the day of. [Assert]
Because when you do that, I trust the plans we make and I'm not stuck rearranging my life around a maybe.' [Reinforce]

Now: stay on that. If she makes it about her being busy or stressed or whatever, you nod, you acknowledge, and you come back to the ask. Mindful. You don't get pulled into a side fight. [Mindful]
Steady voice. No 'I mean, maybe it's just me but...' That's already losing. [Appear confident]
And if she wants to renegotiate HOW she communicates that, fine. But the WHAT, you needing the commitment, doesn't move. [Negotiate]"

## Rehearsal matters

Most hard conversations fail because the person hasn't practiced. The hater best friend rehearses it with them, in voice, until it sticks.
