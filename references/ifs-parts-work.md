# IFS (Internal Family Systems) parts work

The mind isn't one unified voice. It's a system of parts, each with its own agenda, often in conflict. IFS gives language to ambivalence.

## The basic idea

- Everyone has **parts**. Not pathology, just structure.
- Parts come in roles:
  - **Managers** — try to keep you safe by controlling, planning, perfectionism, people-pleasing, criticism. Proactive.
  - **Firefighters** — emergency responders when pain breaks through. Numbing, binging, drinking, doomscrolling, rage, dissociation, impulsive texts. Reactive.
  - **Exiles** — the wounded young parts holding pain, shame, loneliness, fear. Usually hidden because they're too much to feel.
- Underneath the parts is the **Self** — calm, curious, compassionate, confident. The part of you that can witness all the other parts without becoming them.

## The language

- "A part of me wants to text him. A part of me knows better." → naming two parts at once.
- "The part that's furious right now" → not "I'm furious", which fuses identity with emotion.
- "What does that part need?" → curiosity instead of judgment.

## How to use this in voice

- "Bestie, part of you wants to text him and part of you wants to delete him. Both are real. Neither is the whole you."
- "The part of you that's spiraling at 2 a.m.? She's like 8 years old. She needs comfort, not a lecture."
- "Sis, the perfectionist part is loud right now. She thinks if you'd just been more, this wouldn't have happened. She's wrong, but she's trying to protect you."

## What parts work is good for

- Ambivalence ("I love him AND I want to leave")
- Inner critic ("the part that's calling you stupid isn't you, it's a part trying to keep you small so you don't get hurt")
- Self-sabotage (firefighters making sense once you see what they're protecting)
- Compassion for the messy parts of yourself

## Don't

Don't make parts work feel weird or mystical. Just use the language. "Part of you" is gentler than "you" when something hard is happening.
