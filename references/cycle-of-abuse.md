# Cycle of abuse

Lenore Walker's 4-phase model. Explains why people stay in on-again-off-again dynamics and why "but they're so sweet sometimes" is part of the pattern, not a counter-argument to it.

## The four phases

1. **Tension building** — small incidents, walking on eggshells, the victim trying to manage moods, anxiety rising. Can last days, weeks, months.
2. **Incident** — the explosion. Verbal, emotional, financial, physical. The "blow up."
3. **Reconciliation** — the honeymoon. Apologies, gifts, promises to change, "I love you so much", explanations ("I was just stressed", "you know I didn't mean it"). The user remembers why they love them.
4. **Calm** — peace. Everything is good. The user thinks "maybe it's different now." Then tension starts building again.

## Why this matters

- The honeymoon phase is what keeps people stuck. It's not weakness, it's the cycle doing exactly what it's designed to do.
- Each cycle, the calm tends to get shorter and the incidents tend to escalate (though not always linearly).
- The intermittent reinforcement creates trauma bonding (see narcissistic-abuse-patterns).

## Not just romantic

Family relationships, friendships, even workplaces can run this cycle. Substitute "boss" or "mother" for "partner" and the structure holds.

## How to use this in voice

- "Sis, that's the cycle. He blew up, then love-bombed you, then it was calm, and now the tension is building again. You're not crazy, you're reading the pattern."
- "Bestie, the sweet moments aren't evidence it's getting better. The sweet moments are part of the system. That's the whole problem."
- "Queen, ask yourself: how long has the longest 'calm' phase been? Because if it's shrinking, the cycle is intensifying."

## What this skill can and can't do

If the user is describing physical violence, financial control that traps them, or threats to safety: this is the crisis protocol. Drop voice, surface DV resources. The cycle of abuse model is useful for naming the pattern, but the response needs to be safety-first, not catharsis-first.

Hotlines (US): National Domestic Violence Hotline 1-800-799-7233. UK: Refuge 0808 2000 247. Ask the user their region if you don't know.

## When it's just a hard relationship vs. abuse

Hard relationships have conflict. Abusive ones have a cycle. The honeymoon phase, the eggshells, the escalation, the repeated promises that don't hold — these specifically signal abuse pattern, not just incompatibility.
