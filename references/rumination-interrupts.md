# Rumination interrupts

For the 3 a.m. spiral. The replay loop. The "what if I had said" tape that won't stop.

## What rumination is

Repetitive, looping thoughts about a problem without productive movement. Feels like problem-solving. Isn't. Research links rumination to depression maintenance and anxiety amplification.

## Why "just stop thinking about it" doesn't work

Thought suppression backfires (white bear effect). You can't think your way out. You have to interrupt the loop physically, behaviorally, or by redirecting attention.

## Interrupts that actually work

**Physical:**
- Cold water on face, cold shower, ice cube held in hand (activates dive reflex, drops nervous system)
- Vigorous exercise — even 5 minutes
- Bilateral movement (walking, especially outdoors)
- Long exhale breathing (exhale longer than inhale; 4-in, 8-out)

**Cognitive:**
- "Scheduled worry time" — tell the brain "I'll think about this from 7-7:30 p.m., not now." Sounds dumb, works.
- Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can hear, 3 you can touch, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste (5-4-3-2-1 grounding).
- Counting backwards from 100 by 7s.

**Behavioral:**
- Change rooms. Literally move bodies.
- Engage hands with something requiring attention (cooking, puzzle, drawing, even Tetris).
- Call someone. Doesn't have to be about the thing.

**Symbolic:**
- Write the thought down on paper, close the notebook. "I'll come back to you tomorrow."
- Send the unhinged text — to a notes app, not to them.

## How to use this in voice

- "Babe. It's 2 a.m. Your brain is not your friend right now. Cold water on your face. Now. We'll keep talking after."
- "Sis, you've replayed that conversation 40 times tonight. The replay isn't giving you new info. Phone down. Walk to the kitchen. Get water. Come back."
- "Queen, here's the deal. We'll think about him from 8-8:15 tomorrow morning. Right now, you sleep. The thoughts will still be there at 8."

## When rumination becomes something bigger

If it's been weeks of inability to disengage, or it's interfering with sleep / work / eating consistently, that's depression territory and worth flagging: "Hun, this might be more than venting needs. Have you thought about talking to someone with credentials?"
